🔥 Painkiller
Hair-on-fire urgency. ICP pays immediately or uses manual workarounds to solve the problem. Even a rough solution finds instant traction.
🦷 Toothpick
A niggling irritant — not critical, but annoying. Users complain but haven't actively sought a dedicated solution. Coping mechanisms exist.
💊 Vitamin
Nice to have. No urgency. Success requires creating a new behaviour or habit. Users only pay after experiencing the value firsthand.
🔵 Blue Ocean
No direct or indirect competitors. You are creating the category and the need. The market essentially didn't exist before you.
🟠 Orange Ocean
No direct competitors, but indirect alternatives exist — manual processes, workarounds, or different-mechanism solutions serving the same need.
🔴 Red Ocean
At least two direct competitors with 10%+ market share each at time of launch. Search volume for the category is already meaningful.

Painkiller

Developers were wasting days wrangling PayPal/Authorize.net connectors, dealing with PCI compliance burdens and opaque pricing. This was a genuine hair-on-fire problem with clear manual workarounds — classic Painkiller territory.

Market Classification
88%
Orange Ocean

PayPal and Authorize.net existed in 2010 but were consumer/enterprise-first with no developer API. No direct competitor offered API-native payment processing. Indirect competition existed; a direct comparator did not — Orange Ocean.

Key Insight

Stripe didn't enter payments — they entered developer experience. By eliminating PCI burden and enabling integration in minutes, they exploited a wedge incumbents were structurally unable to close.

Playbook

Launch the atomic unit: accept a payment. No subscriptions, no invoicing, no international support — just butter-smooth 7-line integration. Let desperate developers pull you forward.

VC Pitch Angle

Show the manual workarounds developers use today — days of wrangling, PCI burden, opaque pricing. Demonstrate the atomic unit working end-to-end in minutes.

Wedge Opportunity

Developer-first startups who found incumbent payment processors too painful — a segment incumbents were structurally unable to serve due to their enterprise-first model.
